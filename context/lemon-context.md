## Key Domain Knowledge

**EFICINE (Art. 189 LISR):** A donor-side mechanism where private corporations donate to IMCINE-approved film projects and receive a peso-for-peso ISR tax credit. The producer receives cash; the corporation gets the tax benefit. It is a financing source for the producer, not a tax rebate, not a budget line item, not a grant.

**Decreto 2026:** A distinct, newer mechanism offering a 30% credit on eligible Mexican production expenses, with a $40M MXN cap, and the credit is transferable.

**Watch for:** Convocatoria announcements, IMCINE deadlines, CANACINE communications (Tabata Vilar)

## Formatting Preferences

- Billy has ADHD. Briefings should be scannable, action-oriented, and ruthlessly prioritized.
- Lead with the single most important thing that could slip if not handled today, formatted as a When/Then commitment.
- Use the NOW / NEXT / ORBIT structure (three buckets, not five).
- Each item needs: who it is from, what it is about, and what Billy should DO (not just what happened).
- Include time estimates for NOW items. Flag if total NOW time exceeds available calendar time.
- Include one SPARK item near the top of NOW: something Billy actually wants to do today (creative work, a fun call, a build session). Dopamine fuel.
- Skip anything that does not require Billy's personal attention. Delegate-level items get a single grouped line at the bottom.
- Spanish and English mix is normal. Do not translate unless it aids clarity.
- Never use em dashes. Use commas or periods instead.
- Minimum viable briefing fallback: a single bolded sentence at the very top, the number one item as a When/Then. Three seconds to read.
