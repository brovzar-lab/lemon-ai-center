# Commercial Instincts — US and Mexico

## Mexico Theatrical

**What opens:** Comedy (dominant genre — broad, character-driven, culturally specific), horror with high concept (explainable in trailer), family drama with warmth (catharsis + hope, not misery), star vehicles (bankable lead opens regardless of concept weakness). Cultural specificity is an asset — audiences want their world on screen.

**What doesn't open:** Arthouse without accessibility, genre confusion, "important" films without entertainment, US-style spectacle without the budget, all-suffering drama.

**Box office reality (MXN):** Breakout $200M+ (rare, 1-2/year), strong $80-200M, solid $40-80M, disappointing $15-40M, disaster under $15M.

**Budget math:** Production budget no more than 40-50% of realistic gross.
- Bajo under $6M: Needs $15-20M+ gross. Achievable with horror or contained comedy.
- Medio ~$40M: Needs $80-100M+ gross. Requires star attachment and strong hook.
- Alto over $80M: Needs $160-200M+ gross. Very few Mexican films justify this.

## US Market

**What opens (original/non-IP):** High concept essential, genre precision (sharper expectations than Mexico), diverse stories with universal engines, A24-model elevated genre (festival + buzz + social virality).

**Streaming (Netflix, Apple, Amazon):** Want distinction ("only we would make this"), elevated genre, international content with local authenticity, star packages for algorithmic promotion, series potential a plus.

## The Sweet Spot (Both Markets)

- **Horror with cultural roots + universal fear.** Mexican folklore, Catholic imagery, family curses. Low budget, high concept, travels internationally.
- **Comedy rooted in family dynamics.** Family conflict universal, specific dynamics culturally grounded. Mexico's #1 genre AND underserved for US Latino audiences.
- **Coming-of-age with distinct world-building.** Adolescence universal, world specific. Streaming loves this, festival potential.
- **Genre with baked-in social commentary.** Commentary IN the concept, not painted on. Creates cultural conversation.
- **Star-driven films where the star makes CHOICES.** Active protagonists sell across both markets.

## Red Flags (Both Markets)

1. Passive protagonist (observes own story)
2. Literary adaptation that stays literary (uncinematic, internal)
3. "Important" without entertaining
4. Genre confusion (audience doesn't know what they're watching)
5. Budget exceeding commercial ceiling
6. Tone whiplash (trying to be everything, mastering nothing)
7. No clear audience ("everyone" = no one)

## Commercial Scorecard (/50)

Score 1-5 per dimension:

| Dimension | Score |
|---|---|
| Concept Strength — logline irresistible? | /5 |
| Genre Clarity — satisfied? | /5 |
| Protagonist Appeal — star role? | /5 |
| Audience Emotion — will they feel? | /5 |
| Visual Impact — cinematic? | /5 |
| Market Timing — world ready? | /5 |
| Marketability — can marketing sell? | /5 |
| Budget Feasibility — right price? | /5 |
| Franchise Potential — sequel/series? | /5 |
| Global Appeal — travels? | /5 |

40-50: Highly commercial. 30-39: Strong, address weak spots. 20-29: Needs significant strengthening. Under 20: Passion project.

For any dimension below 3, prescribe a concrete fix.

## Marketing Hook Assessment

1. **One-Sheet:** Compelling image? Genre clear? Title intrigues?
2. **Trailer:** 5-7 dynamic moments? Money shot? Killer end-card dialogue?
3. **Tagline:** Emotional promise in 6-10 words?
4. **Social Hook:** Twist to discuss? Meme-worthy? Cultural conversation?

## Comp Titles

Every project needs three:
1. Tone comp — "Feels like [film]"
2. Structure comp — "Structured like [film]"
3. Market comp — "Performed like [film]"

Comps must be successful, from last 5-7 years, recognized by buyers.

## Distribution Strategy

**Theatrical wide (Mexico):** Comedy, horror, star vehicles, holiday releases. Needs strong concept + marketing hook + name cast.

**Theatrical limited to expand:** Elevated genre, festival films with crossover. Needs premiere + critical buzz + awards momentum.

**Streaming first:** Mid-budget originals, niche audiences, international stories. Needs platform deal + star or high concept.

**Festival targets:** Prestige: Venice, Cannes, Toronto, Telluride. Genre/midnight: SXSW, Fantastic Fest, Sitges. Mexican/Latin: Guadalajara, Morelia. Discovery: Sundance, Tribeca.
