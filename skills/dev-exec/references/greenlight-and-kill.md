# Green Light Scorecard and Kill Criteria

## Scorecard Template

```
PROJECT:          [Title]
DATE:             [Date]
STAGE:            [After Pass __]

                                    SCORE(1-5)  WEIGHT  WEIGHTED
Concept Strength / High Concept       [ ]       x3      [  ]
Protagonist (Active, Castable, Arc)   [ ]       x3      [  ]
Commercial Viability (Audience+Math)  [ ]       x3      [  ]
Genre Execution                       [ ]       x2      [  ]
Development Trajectory                [ ]       x2      [  ]
Script Execution                      [ ]       x1      [  ]
Writer Capability                     [ ]       x1      [  ]
                                         TOTAL: [  ]/75

SCORE HISTORY:
Intake: [ ]/75  |  Pass 1: [ ]/75  |  Pass 2: [ ]/75  |  Pass 3: [ ]/75
Trend: [Improving / Flat / Declining]

VERDICT: [ ] Green Light (55-75)  [ ] One More Pass (40-54)  [ ] Stalling (25-39)  [ ] Kill (<25)

ACTION: [Specific next step]
```

## Score Interpretation

**Weight rationale:** Concept, Protagonist, Commercial Viability weighted x3 because they're load-bearing walls — brilliant script with weak concept, passive lead, or no audience is a festival submission at best. Genre and Trajectory at x2 (audience contract + momentum). Execution and Writer at x1 (important but fixable).

**Red flag combinations:**
- Low Concept + High Execution = "Good script, not a movie." Consider concept pivot.
- High Concept + Low Protagonist = "Great poster, boring movie." Fix protagonist first.
- High Everything + Low Commercial = Festival darling. Reassess platform (streamer, not theatrical).
- Declining Trajectory + Any Score = Trend matters more than number. A declining 45 is worse than a rising 35.
- Low Writer + Any Score = Score ceiling is locked. Consider writer change before investing another pass.

## Kill Criteria

Kill or shelve when ANY are true:

**1. Concept doesn't survive scrutiny.**
Test: After development, state the logline to someone new. Do they say "I'd watch that"?
If no: Concept is the problem. Can't rewrite a concept in the margins.

**2. Three passes without meaningful improvement.**
Test: Has the score improved by at least 8 points since intake/Pass 1?
If no: Development stalled. Same notes keep returning. Before killing, verify notes were clear and actionable.

**3. Budget math doesn't work.**
Test: Budget no more than 40-50% of realistic gross (theatrical + ancillary).
Mexico: $40M MXN film needs $80-100M+ gross to justify.

**4. Market moved.**
Test: Similar film released — how did it perform? Differentiate clearly from failure or shelve.

**5. Cannot articulate the audience.**
Test: Complete "This is for [specific audience] because [specific reason they show up opening weekend]."
"Everyone" = no one. Movies open on specific audiences.

**6. Creative energy is dead.**
Test: Is the writer still engaged? Are you protecting sunk cost or genuine potential?
Shelving can save a project — fresh eyes in 6-12 months.

## Kill Communication Template

"After [X passes] of development, [Title] isn't reaching the commercial viability threshold for our slate. The core challenge is [specific reason]. [Writer]'s work on [specific strength] was [genuine compliment], and we'd welcome working together on future projects that [criteria]. Archiving as of [date]."

**Principles:** Be direct and specific about why. Acknowledge what was strong. Don't leave the door open if it's closed. If shelving (not killing), give specific conditions for revisiting.
