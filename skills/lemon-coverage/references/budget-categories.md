# Mexican Film Budget Reference

Budget estimation guidelines for Mexican theatrical productions. All figures in MXN.

## Budget Tiers

| Tier | Range | Typical Projects |
|------|-------|------------------|
| Bajo | <6,000,000 | Contained horror, single-location comedy, micro-budget drama |
| Medio | 6M-60M | Genre films, comedies with known cast, modest action |
| Alto | >80,000,000 | Period pieces, heavy VFX, A-list ensemble, international co-pro |

## Above The Line (ATL) Ranges

### Story/Script
- Bajo: $100,000-300,000
- Medio: $300,000-800,000
- Alto: $800,000-2,000,000+

### Producer Fee
- Bajo: $200,000-400,000
- Medio: $400,000-1,500,000
- Alto: $1,500,000-4,000,000

### Director Fee
- Bajo: $150,000-400,000
- Medio: $400,000-2,000,000
- Alto: $2,000,000-6,000,000+

### Principal Cast (total for leads)
- Bajo: $300,000-800,000 (rising talent)
- Medio: $1,000,000-5,000,000 (known names)
- Alto: $5,000,000-15,000,000+ (A-list)

## Budget Drivers

Factors that increase budget significantly:
- Period setting (+30-50% on art, wardrobe)
- Multiple locations outside CDMX (+20-40%)
- Night shoots (+15-25% overtime)
- Water/weather (+20-30% contingency)
- Animals (+special handlers, insurance)
- Children (limited hours, tutors)
- Stunts/action (+stunt team, safety, insurance)
- Heavy VFX (can be 15-30% of total)
- A-list cast (can be 20-40% of total)
- International co-production (legal, travel, accommodation)

Factors that reduce budget:
- Contemporary setting
- Single/few locations
- Small cast
- CDMX-based shoot
- Practical effects over VFX
- Rising talent vs. established names
- Digital over film

## EFICINE Considerations

If pursuing EFICINE tax incentive:
- Maximum benefit: 10% of production spend (capped at ~$50M MXN benefit)
- Must meet Mexican cultural content requirements
- Auditable costs only
- Plan budget with EFICINE-eligible expenses clearly categorized
