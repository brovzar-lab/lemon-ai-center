# Commercial Analysis Framework

## 1. The "Is It a Movie?" Test

Not every good story is a good MOVIE. Test against cinema-specific requirements.

### The 7 Requirements

| # | Requirement | Test | Pass/Fail |
|---|------------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Visual Promise | Can you describe 3 striking images? | If you can't SEE it, it may be a novel |
| 2 | Time Compression | Can this be told in 90-120 minutes? | If it needs 5 hours, it's a series |
| 3 | Active Protagonist | Does the lead DRIVE the action? | Passive leads = box office poison |
| 4 | Clear Stakes | What specific thing is at risk? | Vague stakes = disengaged audience |
| 5 | Emotional Clarity | What ONE emotion dominates? | Competing emotions = muddy |
| 6 | Ticking Clock | Is there urgency? | No urgency = no tension |
| 7 | Satisfying Ending | Cathartic conclusion? | Ambiguous endings need earned setup |

**Scoring:** 5-7 passes = strong film premise. 3-4 = needs work. Under 3 = may not be a movie.

## 2. Budget Tier Analysis

Know what the screenplay costs before writing it.

### Micro-Budget ($500K — $5M)
- **Constraints:** 1-3 locations, <10 speaking roles, no VFX, no stars
- **Strengths:** High concept overcomes low budget, creative freedom
- **Examples:** *Get Out*, *Paranormal Activity*, *Whiplash*
- **Writing Strategy:** Concept is everything. One location, one weekend, life-or-death stakes
- **Buyers:** Indies, micro-studios, streaming originals

### Low-Budget ($5M — $15M)
- **Constraints:** Limited locations, modest cast, minimal VFX
- **Strengths:** Can attract one recognizable actor, genre-friendly
- **Examples:** *A Quiet Place*, *The Menu*, *Saltburn*
- **Writing Strategy:** Contained thriller/horror, sharp ensemble, one star role + strong supporting
- **Buyers:** A24, Blumhouse, Neon, streaming

### Mid-Budget ($15M — $60M)
- **Constraints:** Need star attachment, clear marketing hook
- **Strengths:** Real production value, can be serious or genre
- **Examples:** *Knives Out*, *The Banshees of Inisherin*
- **Writing Strategy:** Write a role an A-lister wants. Franchise potential helps
- **Buyers:** Mini-majors, studios for prestige, streaming for star packages

### Tentpole ($60M — $300M+)
- **Constraints:** Must be four-quadrant, usually IP-based, franchise potential
- **Strengths:** Massive marketing, global release, cultural event
- **Examples:** MCU, *Dune*, *Barbie*, *Oppenheimer*
- **Writing Strategy:** Build a world, not just a movie. IP adaptation or original event
- **Buyers:** Major studios only

### Budget Impact Checklist
Before writing, assess:
- Number of unique locations (each = money)
- Period vs. contemporary (period = 20-50% more)
- VFX requirements (shots + complexity)
- Stunts/action (coordination = cost)
- Cast size (each speaking role = SAG minimum)
- Animals/children (special considerations)
- Night shoots (premium rates)
- Water/weather (most expensive elements)

## 3. Casting Appeal: The ACTOR Test

| Letter | Question | What Actors Want |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **A** | Agency | Does the character drive the story? |
| **C** | Complexity | Is there emotional range to display? |
| **T** | Transformation | Does the character change meaningfully? |
| **O** | Opportunities | Are there showcase moments (monologues, breakdowns)? |
| **R** | Range | Does the role stretch beyond their usual type? |

### Writing for Castability
- **Showcase Scene:** Every lead needs ONE scene that makes an actor's reel
- **Against-Type Potential:** Let funny actors be serious, action stars be vulnerable
- **"Never Seen This" Factor:** What makes this unlike anything the actor has played?
- **Supporting Role Bait:** Strong supporting roles attract stars for smaller parts

## 4. Marketing Hook Assessment

### The 4 Marketing Pillars

**1. The One-Sheet (Poster)**
- Compelling poster from one image?
- Genre immediately clear?
- Title intrigues?

**2. The Trailer (2.5 minutes)**
- 5-7 visually dynamic moments to cut?
- Three-act mini-story?
- Money shot? (The image everyone shares)
- Killer line of dialogue for the end card?

**3. The Tagline**
- Emotional promise in 6-10 words
- Would someone repeat this to a friend?

**4. The Social Hook**
- Twist people want to discuss?
- Meme-worthy moments?
- Taps into current cultural conversation?

## 5. Genre Marketplace Positioning

### Analysis Dimensions
- **Saturation:** How crowded is this genre right now?
- **Audience appetite:** Growing, stable, or declining?
- **Recent performance:** Top and bottom performers in last 3 years
- **Streaming vs. theatrical:** Where is this genre performing better?
- **Festival trajectory:** Is this genre winning awards?
- **International appeal:** Does it travel globally?
- **White space:** What type of this genre is missing from the market?

### Three Positioning Approaches
1. **Genre-Pure** — Satisfy every convention, compete on execution. Risk: crowded. Reward: clear audience.
2. **Genre-Elevated** — Genre framework aimed higher (A24 model). Risk: "too smart" for genre fans. Reward: critical + commercial.
3. **Genre-Hybrid** — Combine genres for freshness. Risk: confused marketing. Reward: untapped audience.

## 6. Distribution Strategy

### Theatrical vs. Streaming

| Factor | Theatrical | Streaming |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Visual spectacle | ✅ Big screen sells | ❌ Loses impact |
| Star power | ✅ Opens wide | ✅ Algorithm boost |
| Horror/Action | ✅ Communal experience | Depends on concept |
| Intimate drama | ❌ Soft opening | ✅ Discovery over time |
| High concept | ✅ Marketing hook | ✅ Thumbnail appeal |
| Niche audience | ❌ Can't fill theaters | ✅ Finds its audience |

### Key Festivals
- **Sundance** — Discovery, indie darlings, social issue
- **Cannes** — Auteur prestige, international market
- **Venice** — Awards season launchpad
- **Toronto (TIFF)** — Audience award = Oscar predictor
- **SXSW** — Genre, comedy, midnight sections

## 7. The Commercial Scorecard

Rate 1-5 on each dimension:

```
CONCEPT STRENGTH      [  /5] — Logline irresistible?
GENRE CLARITY         [  /5] — Genre clear and satisfied?
PROTAGONIST APPEAL    [  /5] — Would a star want this role?
AUDIENCE EMOTION      [  /5] — Will they FEEL something?
VISUAL IMPACT         [  /5] — Is this cinematic?
MARKET TIMING         [  /5] — Is the world ready?
MARKETABILITY         [  /5] — Can marketing sell this?
BUDGET FEASIBILITY    [  /5] — Can it be made at the right price?
FRANCHISE POTENTIAL   [  /5] — Sequel/series possibility?
GLOBAL APPEAL         [  /5] — Works internationally?

TOTAL                 [  /50]
```

### Scoring Guide
- **40-50**: Highly commercial. Package aggressively.
- **30-39**: Strong potential. Address weak spots.
- **20-29**: Needs significant commercial strengthening.
- **Under 20**: Passion project, not a commercial play.

For any score below 3, prescribe a concrete fix. Provide overall assessment + recommended distribution strategy.

### Comp Title Analysis
Every screenplay needs 2-3 comp titles:
1. **Tone comp** — "It FEELS like [film]"
2. **Structure comp** — "It's STRUCTURED like [film]"
3. **Market comp** — "It PERFORMED like [film]"

Comps must be successful films from the last 5-7 years that buyers recognize but the screenplay isn't directly imitating.
