# Enneagram for Character Development

## The Three Emotional Centers

Before diving into the nine types, understand the three centers that govern them:

**Head Center (5, 6, 7):** Core emotion is FEAR/ANXIETY. These types process through thinking. Concerned with security, guidance, planning.
- Fives respond to fear by withdrawing into thought
- Sixes handle fear with worry, doubt, worst-case planning
- Sevens escape fear by shifting to pleasant scenarios

**Heart Center (2, 3, 4):** Core emotion is SHAME. These types process through feeling. Concerned with identity, image, love.
- Twos respond to shame by seeking confirmation of likability
- Threes respond to shame by proving value through winning
- Fours respond to shame by projecting uniqueness

**Gut/Body Center (8, 9, 1):** Core emotion is ANGER/RAGE. These types process through action/instinct. Concerned with autonomy, justice, control.
- Eights express anger directly and forcefully
- Nines' anger erupts as passive-aggressive behavior
- Ones' anger becomes smoldering resentment and judgment

## The Nine Types

### Type 1: The Reformer

**Core Fear:** Being corrupt, evil, defective, making mistakes
**Core Desire:** To have integrity, be good, be balanced
**Core Flaw:** Resentment (anger they won't admit)
**Poison:** Fear of change/chaos → Resentment → Self-loathing/defectiveness
**Distortion:** Desire for integrity → Critical perfectionism and intolerance

**Focus of Attention:** What is right or wrong, correct or incorrect
**Survival Strategy:** Stay safe by following the rules; get approval of authority
**Core Image:** "I am reasonable"

**Character Traits:** Principled, purposeful, self-controlled, perfectionist, critical, judgmental, inflexible
**Want:** To fix what's wrong, achieve perfection
**Need:** To accept imperfection, find serenity

**Under Stress (→4):** Becomes moody, irrational, withdraws into fantasy of control
**In Growth (→7):** Becomes spontaneous, joyful, appreciates beauty of life

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Precise, direct, exacting. Uses "should," "ought," "must," "correct," "right," "wrong"
- Blind Spots: Appears critical, impatient, angry. Tenacious about own opinions
- Distortion Filters: Preoccupied with whether others behave correctly

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Being criticized, another's lack of follow-through, changes to a plan, feeling deceived
- Pinch Behaviors: Curt statements, accusations about other issues, nonverbal anger cues, saying nothing

**Story Low Point:** Becomes obsessed with perfection, rules, righteously judgmental. Entrenched in fear about not doing things correctly. Falls into a Four's fantasy of micromanaging chaos.

**Archetypes:** Crusader, perfectionist, critic, reformer
**Example Characters:** Atticus Finch, Hermione Granger, Dolores Umbridge, Thomas More (A Man for All Seasons)

---

### Type 2: The Helper

**Core Fear:** Being unwanted, unloved, worthless, unneeded
**Core Desire:** To feel loved, needed, appreciated
**Core Flaw:** Pride (believing they alone know what others need)
**Poison:** Fear of being unlovable → Pride → Self-deception, overvaluing self
**Distortion:** Desire for love → Insatiable need to be needed, controlling manipulation

**Focus of Attention:** Will others like me? Will I be needed?
**Survival Strategy:** Find the one with power and make them love/need me
**Core Image:** "I am loving, helpful"

**Character Traits:** Generous, demonstrative, people-pleasing, possessive, manipulative through giving
**Want:** To be loved and appreciated
**Need:** To love themselves, acknowledge own needs

**Under Stress (→8):** Becomes aggressive, dominating, confrontational, controlling
**In Growth (→4):** Becomes self-aware, emotionally honest, experiences full range of emotion

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Asks questions, gives compliments, focuses on other person. Few self-references
- Blind Spots: Hidden intentions beneath generosity. Disengages quickly if uninterested
- Distortion Filters: Whether other person likes them, whether they want to help them

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Being taken for granted, feeling unappreciated, not being heard
- Pinch Behaviors: Keep feelings hidden long, then intensely emotional. Pre-plan confrontations

**Story Low Point:** Lashes out or controls the person most important to them. "I'm going to make you love me." Degenerates into Eightish control and domination.

**Archetypes:** Caretaker, enabler, martyr, manipulator
**Example Characters:** Samwise Gamgee, Molly Weasley, Eve Harrington (All About Eve), Norma Desmond

---

### Type 3: The Achiever

**Core Fear:** Being worthless, without value apart from achievements
**Core Desire:** To feel valuable and worthwhile
**Core Flaw:** Deceit (self-deception about who they really are)
**Poison:** Fear of no value → Vanity → Self-delusion about superiority
**Distortion:** Desire to matter → Resentment toward approval sources, achievement stops

**Focus of Attention:** How can I gain respect and esteem of others?
**Survival Strategy:** Get approval of as many people as possible by winning
**Core Image:** "I am outstanding, a winner"

**Character Traits:** Adaptable, driven, image-conscious, competitive, chameleon-like, workaholic
**Want:** Success, admiration, status
**Need:** To know their inherent worth beyond achievements

**Under Stress (→9):** Becomes disengaged, apathetic, numb, achievement stops
**In Growth (→6):** Becomes committed, loyal, authentic, achieves for others' security

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Clear, efficient, logical. Quick on feet. Avoids topics reflecting poorly on them
- Blind Spots: Impatient with less capable people. Avoids discussing own failings. Seems rushed
- Distortion Filters: Whether info makes them look good, confidence of other person

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Position of likely failure, not looking good, blamed for others' poor work, not receiving credit
- Pinch Behaviors: Short structured questions, unlikely to show upset, sharp tone over time

**Story Low Point:** Caught in lethargy of "I don't matter, nothing matters." Resents people they've tried to win over. Falls into Nineish paralysis of self-loathing.

**Archetypes:** Performer, status seeker, shapeshifter, success machine
**Example Characters:** Don Draper, Tom Ripley, Mitch McDeere (The Firm), Frank Galvin (The Verdict at start)

---

### Type 4: The Individualist

**Core Fear:** Having no identity, being ordinary, personal insignificance
**Core Desire:** To find themselves and their significance
**Core Flaw:** Envy (always seeing what's missing)
**Poison:** Fear of ordinariness → Envy → Self-absorbed, clingy, needy
**Distortion:** Desire for wholeness → Neurotic self-absorption, searching for "the one" to fix them

**Focus of Attention:** What is missing? What do others have that I lack?
**Survival Strategy:** Be special, be the exception, find someone to complete me
**Core Image:** "I am unique, different from others"

**Character Traits:** Expressive, dramatic, self-absorbed, temperamental, melancholic, creative
**Want:** To be unique, to be understood
**Need:** To accept ordinary happiness, find equanimity

**Under Stress (→2):** Becomes clingy, over-involved, desperate for help and care
**In Growth (→1):** Becomes principled, disciplined, objective, creates something special

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Uses "I," "me," "my," "mine" frequently. Discusses feelings. Shares painful stories
- Blind Spots: Pulls conversation back to self. Needs to fully complete discussions. Appears dramatic
- Distortion Filters: Personal rejection, being slighted, not wanting to appear defective

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Being ignored or slighted, asked to do something contrary to values, events triggering envy
- Pinch Behaviors: Blunt statements, becomes extremely quiet, multiple intense feelings, holds on long

**Story Low Point:** Becomes overly involved, needy, desperate to get someone to help or care. Falls into Twoish overwhelming neediness and obsessive focus on making others notice.

**Archetypes:** Artist, romantic, tragic figure, outsider
**Example Characters:** Fleabag, Blanche Dubois, Antonio Salieri (Amadeus), Ronnie Cammereri (Moonstruck)

---

### Type 5: The Investigator

**Core Fear:** Being useless, helpless, incapable, overwhelmed
**Core Desire:** To be capable and competent
**Core Flaw:** Avarice (hoarding resources—time, energy, knowledge)
**Poison:** Fear of incompetence → Avarice → Panic, helplessness, compulsive wanting
**Distortion:** Desire for competence → Useless specialization to feel accomplished

**Focus of Attention:** How does this work? What do I need to understand?
**Survival Strategy:** Withdraw into mind, figure things out, minimize needs
**Core Image:** "I am perceptive, wise"

**Character Traits:** Perceptive, innovative, secretive, isolated, detached, cerebral
**Want:** Knowledge, understanding, privacy
**Need:** To engage with the world, share themselves

**Under Stress (→7):** Becomes scattered, impulsive, escapist, abandons thinking for numbness
**In Growth (→8):** Becomes confident, decisive, engaged, brings knowledge into the world

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Terse or lengthy discourse. Selective words. Shares thoughts not feelings
- Blind Spots: May not exhibit warmth. Appears aloof. May say too much or too little
- Distortion Filters: Others' demands, feeling inadequate, intense emotions, physical proximity

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Breaking confidences, being surprised, dishonesty, out-of-control situations
- Pinch Behaviors: Say little, pull back, retain experience mentally, may express anger as outrage

**Story Low Point:** When thinking fails, runs from pain, abandons thinking for excess or obsession. Falls into Sevenish numbness and escape, unable to think clearly.

**Archetypes:** Observer, expert, hermit, detached analyst
**Example Characters:** Sherlock Holmes, Dr. Hannibal Lecter, George (Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf)

---

### Type 6: The Loyalist

**Core Fear:** Being without support and guidance, alone and unsafe
**Core Desire:** To have security and support
**Core Flaw:** Fear/Anxiety (scanning for threats)
**Poison:** Fear of abandonment → Anxiety → Reactive, competitive, torpor
**Distortion:** Desire for security → Worry, catastrophizing, paralysis of inaction

**Focus of Attention:** What could go wrong? Who can I trust?
**Survival Strategy:** Find reliable authority, prepare for worst-case scenarios
**Core Image:** "I am loyal, reliable"

**Character Traits:** Committed, security-oriented, anxious, suspicious, loyal, defensive, reactive
**Want:** Security, certainty, reliable allies
**Need:** To trust themselves, find inner courage

**Under Stress (→3):** Becomes competitive, arrogant, desperate to get a win
**In Growth (→9):** Becomes relaxed, trusting, accepting, finds peace

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Starts analytical, alternates hesitant with bold, discusses worries and "what ifs"
- Blind Spots: Negativism appears as pessimism. Self-doubt causes others to question competence
- Distortion Filters: Proper/improper use of authority, projection onto others, trust issues

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Pressure, lack of genuineness, lack of commitment, abusive authority
- Pinch Behaviors: May withdraw, intensive analysis, highly reactive, projects own feelings onto others

**Story Low Point:** Tries to please all sources of fear, then becomes hostile toward those sources. Falls into Threeish desperation to get any win and find something trustworthy.

**Archetypes:** Devil's advocate, loyalist, skeptic, troubleshooter
**Example Characters:** Hamlet, Chidi Anagonye, Norman Bates (at extremity)

---

### Type 7: The Enthusiast

**Core Fear:** Being deprived, trapped in pain, missing out
**Core Desire:** To be satisfied and content
**Core Flaw:** Gluttony (for experiences, avoiding pain)
**Poison:** Fear of pain → Gluttony → Excess, critical perfectionism, smugness
**Distortion:** Desire for happiness → Frenetic escapism, addiction, distraction

**Focus of Attention:** What's next? What are the possibilities?
**Survival Strategy:** Stay positive, keep options open, avoid pain by moving fast
**Core Image:** "I am happy, exciting"

**Character Traits:** Spontaneous, versatile, scattered, acquisitive, escapist, excessive, undisciplined
**Want:** Freedom, happiness, stimulation
**Need:** To sit with discomfort, find depth in the present

**Under Stress (→1):** Becomes critical, perfectionist, rigid, righteously blaming
**In Growth (→5):** Becomes focused, profound, satisfied with less, savors solitude

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Quick, spontaneous, words in flurry. Engaging stories. Shifts topics. Upbeat. Reframes negatives
- Blind Spots: May not have absorbed info they claim. Behavior causes others not to take them seriously
- Distortion Filters: Having competence demeaned, thinks they know what others will say, limits being placed

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Boring mundane tasks, feeling dismissed, unjust criticism
- Pinch Behaviors: Avoids by thinking of pleasurable alternatives, rationalizes, blames others

**Story Low Point:** Controls excess by becoming perfect and constrained, righteous and blaming. Stuck in Oneish judgment and rigid agony.

**Archetypes:** Adventurer, hedonist, eternal child, escapist
**Example Characters:** Peter Pan, Ferris Bueller, Andre Gregory (My Dinner with Andre), Larry Rhodes (A Face in the Crowd)

---

### Type 8: The Challenger

**Core Fear:** Being harmed or controlled by others, showing weakness
**Core Desire:** To protect themselves, control their own life
**Core Flaw:** Lust (for intensity, control, excess)
**Poison:** Fear of weakness → Lust → Defensive power obsession, excess, numbing
**Distortion:** Desire for self-reliance → Constant fighting and emotional combat

**Focus of Attention:** Who has power here? Is this fair/just?
**Survival Strategy:** Take control, establish dominance, never show weakness
**Core Image:** "I am strong, powerful"

**Character Traits:** Self-confident, decisive, willful, confrontational, dominating, aggressive
**Want:** Control, strength, justice (their version)
**Need:** To show vulnerability, surrender to love

**Under Stress (→5):** Becomes secretive, withdrawn, paranoid, isolates
**In Growth (→2):** Becomes nurturing, protective, open-hearted, gives to others

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Structures/controls situations. Impatient with detail. Raises intensity until response. May use profanity
- Blind Spots: Many are intimidated by them. Energy stronger than realized. Vulnerability may show
- Distortion Filters: Protecting those who need it, weakness in others, control issues, truthfulness

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Injustice, not dealing directly with issues, others not taking responsibility, being blindsided
- Pinch Behaviors: Surges of anger propelling action, quick sifting of info, avoids feeling vulnerable, dismisses disrespected people

**Story Low Point:** Takes charge and control of everything and everyone. After repeated defeats and exposures of weakness, becomes punishingly controlling or rejects everyone. Falls into Fiveish paranoia and analysis-paralysis.

**Archetypes:** Protector, boss, tyrant, challenger
**Example Characters:** Tony Soprano, Michael Corleone, Principal Joe Clark (Lean on Me)

---

### Type 9: The Peacemaker

**Core Fear:** Loss and separation, fragmentation, conflict
**Core Desire:** To have inner stability, peace of mind
**Core Flaw:** Sloth (in attending to own priorities and development)
**Poison:** Fear of separation → Sloth → Lethargy, pathological self-abandonment
**Distortion:** Desire for peace → Giving up self to avoid conflict, merging with others

**Focus of Attention:** What do others want? How can we all get along?
**Survival Strategy:** Go along to get along, merge with others' agendas, stay comfortable
**Core Image:** "I am peaceful, easygoing"

**Character Traits:** Receptive, reassuring, agreeable, complacent, resigned, stubborn passively
**Want:** Peace, harmony, comfort
**Need:** To wake up, engage, claim their own life

**Under Stress (→6):** Becomes anxious, worried, reactive, frozen in fear
**In Growth (→3):** Becomes energetic, focused, self-developing, productive

**Communication Style:**
- Speaking: Highly detailed, sequential. Presents all sides. May say yes but mean no. Uses filler words
- Blind Spots: Prolonged explanations lose listeners. Multiple viewpoints affect credibility. Doesn't make wants known
- Distortion Filters: Demands to change, being criticized/ignored, opposing views, anger directed at them

**Conflict Style:**
- Common Pinches: Disruption of harmony, being told what to do, feeling ignored, rudeness, hostility, being confronted
- Pinch Behaviors: Say nothing, slight facial tension, may be unaware of own anger, displaces anger, anger lingers

**Story Low Point:** Becomes frozen in fear like deer in headlights, unable to act. Falls into Sixish anxiety where peace is no longer possible and they can't even speak.

**Archetypes:** Mediator, peacekeeper, invisible one, comforter
**Example Characters:** The Dude, Forrest Gump, Wally Shawn (My Dinner with Andre)

## The Moral Component

Every protagonist needs a MORAL COMPONENT—the engine that drives conflict and change. It consists of three elements:

### 1. Moral Blind Spot

A core belief the protagonist holds that twists their moral compass and poisons external relationships. Generated by a BASE FEAR about themselves, to which they are blind but which colors all choices, decisions, attitudes, and actions—resulting in hurting other people.

The protagonist:
- Knows their core belief (superficially)
- Is blind to their base fear
- Is in denial about negative impact on others

### 2. Immoral Effect

The moral blind spot in ACTION. The bad behavior the protagonist exhibits that hurts others. Not internal angst—visible behavior on the page that damages relationships.

**Key insight:** All immoral behavior comes from the need to CONTROL—either through domination/intimidation OR through weakness/victimhood. Two sides of the same coin.

### 3. Dynamic Moral Tension

The engine that keeps conflict running. Throughout the story, the protagonist faces serious moral choices—not vanilla vs. chocolate, but: cheat on spouse or not, kill this person or not, rob the bank or not.

These choices stem from the blind spot and immoral effect. The protagonist is repeatedly offered chances to change but refuses until they've been through enough failure.

## Active vs. Passive Protagonist

### Passive Protagonist Loop (Situations)
```
Problem finds protagonist → Reactive choice → Reactive effect → (loop continues until problem solved)
```
The protagonist didn't create the problem. They react to external events. This creates EPISODIC writing.

### Active Protagonist Loop (Stories)
```
Moral Blind Spot → Immoral Effect → Problem/Consequence → Proactive Choice → Proactive Effect → Offer to Change → Refusal → (loop continues) → Truth Moment → New Moral Choice → New Moral Effect
```
The protagonist CREATES their problems through their blind spot. They are the unwitting architect of their own fall. This creates NARRATIVE DRIVE.

---

## The Protagonist Change Triangle

For any protagonist, define three states based on their Enneagram:

### START (Top of Triangle)
Who are they at the story's beginning? How does their moral flaw manifest?
- Based on their type's SURVIVAL STRATEGY and FOCUS OF ATTENTION
- Operating from core fear, using defense mechanisms

### EVOLUTION (Bottom Left)
Where do they end up if they GROW?
- Based on their type's SECURITY/GROWTH direction
- The positive qualities they gain
- Their need is met, not just their want

### DE-EVOLUTION (Bottom Right)
Where do they end up if they DECLINE?
- Based on their type's STRESS/FEAR direction
- The negative qualities that consume them
- Their flaw fully manifests, consequences arrive

---

## Pattern of Decline (Story Middle)

The protagonist's journey from Inciting Incident to Doom Moment:

```
Immoral Effect → Problem/Consequence → Proactive Choice → Proactive Effect → Offer to Change → REFUSAL → (new Problem/Consequence) → (loop repeats, stakes rising each time) → DOOM MOMENT
```

Each pass through the loop:
- Stakes rise
- Relationships fracture
- Protagonist moves closer to their de-evolution point
- They exhibit more of the negative qualities of their stress type

**The Doom Moment accomplishes TWO things:**
1. Emotional devastation (internal)
2. Physical isolation (external—allies gone, resources depleted)

---

## Pattern of Elevation (Story Third Act)

The protagonist's journey from Doom Moment to Resolution:

1. **Doom Moment:** Rock bottom emotionally and materially
2. **First Awakening:** "I can't keep going this way. Something has to change."
3. **First Step Toward Change:** Action in the right direction, now on growth path
4. **Second Awakening:** "I can't do this alone."
5. **Reconnection in a New Way:** Returns to allies with humility, asks for help
6. **Final Awakening:** Through conversation/intervention, finally SEES the blind spot
7. **Moment of Truth:** Final test—choose to heal blind spot and defeat opponent, or fold

## Wings: Adding Complexity

Each type is colored by adjacent types. A character accesses wing qualities based on FEAR or SAFETY in the moment.

### Wing Dynamics by Type

**1w9 vs 1w2:**
- Safety: 1w9 more calm, cooperative, discerning / 1w2 more giving, helpful
- Fear: 1w9 less present, passive-aggressive / 1w2 micromanages, harsher judgments

**2w1 vs 2w3:**
- Safety: 2w1 more serious, altruistic / 2w3 comfortable in spotlight, goal-oriented
- Fear: 2w1 quick to judge, stringent standards / 2w3 obsessive about image, strategic helping

**3w2 vs 3w4:**
- Safety: 3w2 more people-focused, empathic / 3w4 more authentic, sensitive to moods
- Fear: 3w2 approval-seeking magnified / 3w4 moodier, inflated importance

**4w3 vs 4w5:**
- Safety: 4w3 more energetic, less ruminative / 4w5 more logical, less impulsive
- Fear: 4w3 less genuine, smoke over substance / 4w5 deeper withdrawal, feeling marginal

**5w4 vs 5w6:**
- Safety: 5w4 balance thinking/feeling, more involved / 5w6 stronger presence, committed
- Fear: 5w4 deeper withdrawal, moodiness / 5w6 more paranoid, worst-case thinking

**6w5 vs 6w7:**
- Safety: 6w5 trusts inner voice, more objective / 6w7 more optimistic, quicker thinking
- Fear: 6w5 escalated fear, lives in head / 6w7 more avoidant, deflects with plans

**7w6 vs 7w8:**
- Safety: 7w6 more deliberative, works with group / 7w8 more assertive, moves to action
- Fear: 7w6 exaggerated fears, erratic / 7w8 uses others, haughty, aggressive

**8w7 vs 8w9:**
- Safety: 8w7 big-picture view, engages others / 8w9 softer, allows events to unfold
- Fear: 8w7 less self-aware, addictive / 8w9 loses inner confidence, dissociated

**9w8 vs 9w1:**
- Safety: 9w8 more decisive, autonomous / 9w1 more structured, principled
- Fear: 9w8 explosive anger, aggressive / 9w1 obsesses over "right," passive-aggressive
