# Building the Opposition

## The Main Opponent Defined

A main opponent is NOT just "the bad guy." They are:

1. **A supporting character** who serves the protagonist's journey (through conflict)
2. **Human, not abstract** - not a concept, force of nature, or "the protagonist's inner self"
3. **The protagonist's dark mirror** - reflecting what they could become if they don't change

## The Opponent's Seven Functions

1. **Does not need the protagonist** and wants to stop them / beat them to the goal
   - OR needs the protagonist to achieve a mutual win, but on the opponent's terms

2. **Can be known or stranger** - but personal connection creates richer conflict

3. **Has the protagonist's de-evolution Enneagram style** - they ARE the cautionary tale

4. **Mirrors the protagonist's moral component** - similar immoral effects, different perspective

5. **Preys on the protagonist's vulnerabilities** - communication blind spots, conflict pinches, fears

6. **Directly contributes to protagonist's growth or destruction**

7. **Can change or not change** - but changing opponents are more interesting

## The Enneagram Opponent Triangle

A tool for designing opposition based on your protagonist's Enneagram style.

```
        PATTERN OF DECLINE              OPPONENT PROFILE
        (How protagonist falls)    ←→   (Who opponent IS)
                    \                    /
                     \                  /
                      \                /
                       \              /
                        \            /
                         CONSEQUENCE
                    (What happens if no change)
```

### Pattern of Decline (Top Left)
How the protagonist makes bad choices, becomes emotionally constricted, and undermines relationships through the middle of the story.

Based on:
- Core Fear → Poison
- Core Desire → Distortion
- De-evolution direction
- Communication blind spots
- Conflict pinches

### Opponent Profile (Top Right)
The lowest expression of the protagonist's de-evolution point. This is who the protagonist will become if they don't change—and who the opponent already is.

**Key insight:** The opponent goes after the protagonist's Enneagram vulnerabilities:
- Communication blind spots
- Distortion filters
- Common and uncommon conflict pinches
- Core fears

### Consequence (Bottom)
The logical emotional, mental, and behavioral outcome if the protagonist doesn't change.

Two components:
- **Character consequence:** The emotional victory of the moral blind spot (Enneagram-based)
- **Action consequence:** How this plays out in scenes (story-specific)

## Opponent Triangles by Protagonist Type

### If Protagonist is a ONE

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of mistakes → Resentment → Self-loathing
- Desire for integrity → Critical perfectionism → Intolerance
- Communication: Appears critical, impatient, angry
- Pinches: Criticism, lack of follow-through, changes to plans, deception

**Opponent Profile (FOUR at worst):**
- Self-absorbed, dramatic, entitled
- Wallows in feelings, withdraws into fantasy
- Envious, believes they deserve special treatment
- Attacks One's need for order with chaos and emotional excess

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist becomes obsessed with perfection, micromanaging reality
- Retreats into righteous judgment and fantasy of control
- Loses relationships through criticism and inflexibility

---

### If Protagonist is a TWO

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of being unloved → Pride → Self-deception
- Desire for love → Insatiable neediness → Controlling manipulation
- Communication: Hidden intentions beneath generosity
- Pinches: Being taken for granted, unappreciated, not heard

**Opponent Profile (EIGHT at worst):**
- Dominating, controlling, aggressive
- "I'll make you love me" through force
- Uses power to get compliance, not genuine connection
- Attacks Two's need for appreciation by withholding it

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist becomes controlling and smothering
- Lashes out at or dominates the person most important to them
- Love becomes possession

---

### If Protagonist is a THREE

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of worthlessness → Vanity → Self-delusion about superiority
- Desire to matter → Resentment toward approval sources → Achievement stops
- Communication: Avoids topics reflecting poorly on them
- Pinches: Likely failure, not looking good, blamed for others' work, no credit

**Opponent Profile (NINE at worst):**
- Disengaged, apathetic, numb
- "Nothing matters, why try?"
- Passive-aggressive, refuses to act
- Attacks Three's need for achievement by making everything pointless

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist falls into lethargy and self-loathing
- Resents people they've tried to impress
- Achievement becomes meaningless, paralysis sets in

---

### If Protagonist is a FOUR

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of ordinariness → Envy → Self-absorption
- Desire for wholeness → Neurotic searching for "the one" to fix them
- Communication: Pulls conversation to self, dramatic, needs completion
- Pinches: Being ignored/slighted, contrary to values, envy triggers

**Opponent Profile (TWO at worst):**
- Clingy, desperate, manipulative through "helping"
- "I need you to need me"
- Smothering, controlling through care
- Attacks Four's uniqueness by making them dependent

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist becomes overly involved, needy, desperate
- Obsesses over getting someone to help or care
- Loses self in pursuit of external validation

---

### If Protagonist is a FIVE

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of incompetence → Avarice → Panic and helplessness
- Desire for competence → Useless specialization → Withdrawal
- Communication: May not show warmth, aloof, too much or too little
- Pinches: Breaking confidences, surprises, dishonesty, out-of-control situations

**Opponent Profile (SEVEN at worst):**
- Scattered, impulsive, escapist
- Abandons thinking for numbness and excess
- Avoids depth, runs from pain
- Attacks Five's need for understanding by overwhelming with chaos

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist's thinking fails them
- Runs from pain into distraction and excess
- Can't think clearly, loses competence they valued

---

### If Protagonist is a SIX

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of abandonment → Anxiety → Reactive, competitive, frozen
- Desire for security → Catastrophizing → Paralysis
- Communication: Hesitant then bold, discusses worries, projects feelings
- Pinches: Pressure, lack of genuineness, lack of commitment, abusive authority

**Opponent Profile (THREE at worst):**
- Desperate to win at any cost
- Image over substance
- Competitive, arrogant, deceitful
- Attacks Six's need for security by being untrustworthy

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist tries to please all sources of fear
- Becomes hostile toward those sources when pleasing fails
- Desperate for any win, any authority to trust

---

### If Protagonist is a SEVEN

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of pain → Gluttony → Excess, critical perfectionism
- Desire for happiness → Frenetic escapism → Addiction, distraction
- Communication: Quick, shifts topics, reframes negatives
- Pinches: Boring tasks, being dismissed, unjust criticism

**Opponent Profile (ONE at worst):**
- Critical, perfectionist, rigid
- Righteously blaming others
- Constrained, joyless, obsessed with rules
- Attacks Seven's freedom by imposing structure and judgment

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist controls excess by becoming constrained
- Trapped in rigid perfectionism and blame
- The very prison they feared

---

### If Protagonist is an EIGHT

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of weakness → Lust → Power obsession, numbing
- Desire for self-reliance → Constant fighting → Emotional combat
- Communication: Controls situations, raises intensity until response
- Pinches: Injustice, not dealing directly, others not taking responsibility, blindsided

**Opponent Profile (FIVE at worst):**
- Secretive, withdrawn, paranoid
- Isolated, trusts no one
- Overthinks, can't act
- Attacks Eight's need for control by exposing vulnerabilities

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist takes punishing control of everything
- After repeated defeats, rejects everyone
- Paranoid isolation, analysis paralysis

---

### If Protagonist is a NINE

**Pattern of Decline:**
- Fear of separation → Sloth → Self-abandonment
- Desire for peace → Giving up self → Merging with others
- Communication: Detailed, presents all sides, may say yes but mean no
- Pinches: Disruption of harmony, told what to do, ignored, confronted

**Opponent Profile (SIX at worst):**
- Anxious, worried, reactive
- Frozen in fear, unable to act
- Paranoid about threats
- Attacks Nine's peace by creating constant anxiety

**Consequence:**
- Protagonist becomes frozen like deer in headlights
- Peace no longer possible
- Can't even speak or act, paralyzed by fear

## Seven Questions for Finding Your Opponent

When testing potential characters for main opponent status:

1. Who in the story is best situated to leverage the protagonist's pattern of decline?

2. Who comes closest to the protagonist's de-evolution Enneagram style?

3. Who will benefit most from the consequence coming to fruition?

4. Who will be the central force battling the protagonist for control in the end?

5. Who has the most to win if the protagonist loses?

6. Who best matches the opponent profile in the Enneagram Opponent Triangle?

7. Who creates the most direct dramatic or comedic conflict for the protagonist?

## Points of Attack by Opponent Type

If your opponent is this type, these are their natural weapons:

| Opponent Type | Primary Weapons |
|--------------|-----------------|
| ONE | Criticism, judgment, exposing imperfection, rigid standards |
| TWO | Withholding love, making protagonist feel unworthy, smothering |
| THREE | Competition, making protagonist feel like a failure, image attacks |
| FOUR | Emotional manipulation, making protagonist feel ordinary, drama |
| FIVE | Withholding information, exposing incompetence, creating chaos |
| SIX | Creating doubt, exposing disloyalty, threatening security |
| SEVEN | Trapping, limiting options, forcing pain, imposing boredom |
| EIGHT | Domination, exposing weakness, taking control, intimidation |
| NINE | Passive aggression, withdrawing support, creating conflict |

## Opponents Who Change

The best opponent stories feature antagonists who transform:

**Perception Reversal:** We think they're bad, but they're actually good (or vice versa)
- Severus Snape (Harry Potter)
- T-800 (Terminator 2)
- Godzilla (2014)

**Actual Character Change:** The opponent genuinely transforms through interaction with protagonist
- Miranda Priestly (The Devil Wears Prada)
- Darth Vader (Return of the Jedi)

The second type is harder but more satisfying. Both opponent and protagonist change because of their relationship.

## The Opponent's Moral Component

Just as your protagonist has a moral blind spot and immoral effect, so should your opponent.

**The best opponent pairings share similar immoral effects from different perspectives.**

Example - The Dark Knight:
- Batman (Five presenting as Eight): Uses vigilante violence to punish the "guilty" (criminals)
- Joker (Seven): Uses chaos and violence to punish the "guilty" (hypocrites)

Same immoral effect (violence, operating above the law), different moral justification. This creates profound emotional connection between hero and villain—they're disturbingly similar.

**Key question:** What would your opponent have to believe about themselves to justify their bad behavior? That belief should mirror or contrast with your protagonist's blind spot.
