# Research Methods for Screenwriters

## 1. Genre Intelligence Research

Know the rules before breaking them.

### Genre Convention Matrix

For any genre, research these 5 dimensions:

| Dimension | What to Find | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Conventions | Required story elements | Meeting audience expectations |
| Obligatory Scenes | Scenes the genre demands | Satisfying the genre contract |
| Recent Hits (3 years) | What's working NOW | Market currency |
| Recent Misses | What flopped and why | Avoid mistakes |
| Innovation Zone | Where conventions are being bent | Opportunity space |

### Full Genre Research Template

Research for the specific genre:
- **Conventions:** 8-10 elements audiences expect
- **Obligatory Scenes:** Must-have scenes
- **Recent Hits (3 years):** 5 films, their hook, box office
- **Recent Misses (3 years):** 3 underperformers and why
- **Innovation Opportunities:** Where is this genre ready for reinvention?
- **Audience Profile:** Demographics + psychographics
- **Tone Spectrum:** Range from lightest to darkest

### Subgenre Deep-Dives

Research the SPECIFIC subgenre, not just the parent:
- **Thriller:** Psychological, Political, Techno, Erotic, Legal, Conspiracy
- **Horror:** Slasher, Supernatural, Psychological, Body, Folk, Cosmic
- **Comedy:** Rom-Com, Dark, Buddy, Satire, Farce, Coming-of-Age
- **Action:** Heist, Revenge, Survival, Espionage, Martial Arts, Disaster

## 2. Market Landscape Analysis

### The Buyer Landscape

| Tier | Typical Budget | What Sells | Current Appetite |
|------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Streaming (Netflix, Apple, Amazon) | $5-200M | IP, star packages, four-quadrant | High-concept genre |
| Major Studios | $50-300M | Franchise, sequel, IP | Known brands |
| Mini-Majors (Lionsgate, A24, Neon) | $5-30M | Auteur-driven, genre-elevated | Festival heat + commercial hook |
| Independents | $1-10M | High concept, contained, castable | Fresh voices, limited locations |

### Market Research Template
- **Comp Titles:** 5 similar films from last 5 years with performance data
- **Buyer Targets:** Which studios/streamers most interested and why
- **Budget Tier:** Realistic range for this concept
- **Talent Targets:** 5 actors who'd want this lead and why
- **Timing:** Market hungry or saturated?
- **Packaging Strategy:** What elements make this a package?

### Comp Title Protocol
Every screenplay needs 2-3 comp titles:
1. **Tone comp** — "It FEELS like [film]"
2. **Structure comp** — "It's STRUCTURED like [film]"
3. **Market comp** — "It PERFORMED like [film]"

Comps must be successful, recognizable, from last 5-7 years. Not direct imitations.

## 3. Subject Matter Research

### Six Layers of Authenticity

1. **Surface Details** — Terminology, uniforms, locations, tools of the trade
2. **Daily Rhythms** — What does a typical day look like?
3. **Professional Protocols** — Procedures, hierarchies, rules (written and unwritten)
4. **Insider Knowledge** — What does only someone in this world know?
5. **Emotional Reality** — What does it FEEL like to live in this world?
6. **Conflict Zones** — Where do tensions naturally emerge?

### Research Template
- **Vocabulary:** 20 terms/jargon an insider uses naturally
- **Daily Life:** Typical day, routines, rituals
- **Hierarchy:** Power structure, ranks, pecking order
- **Insider Details:** 10 things only insiders know
- **Emotional Terrain:** Common frustrations, fears, satisfactions
- **Natural Conflicts:** Where tensions arise
- **Visual Texture:** Sensory details
- **Common Misconceptions:** What movies usually get wrong

### Verification Protocol
AI research provides a strong foundation but requires verification for:
- Legal procedures (jurisdiction matters)
- Medical details (accuracy = credibility)
- Military protocols (branch-specific)
- Technical processes (step-by-step accuracy)
- Cultural specifics (sensitivity + authenticity)

**Rule:** If it would embarrass you in front of an expert, verify it.

## 4. Character Authenticity Research

### Occupational Research
For any character with a specific profession:
- **How They Talk:** Speech patterns, vocabulary, verbal tics
- **How They Think:** Decision-making framework, priorities, biases
- **How They Carry Themselves:** Body language, physicality, presence
- **What They Fear:** Professional and personal anxieties
- **What They Hide:** Common secrets in this profession
- **Status Markers:** How insiders signal hierarchy/competence
- **Deformation Professionnelle:** How the job warps personality
- **Daily Irritations:** Small frustrations specific to the role

### Speech Pattern Research
For region/background-specific voice:
- **Syntax:** Sentence structure patterns
- **Vocabulary:** Region/class-specific word choices
- **Rhythm:** Pacing and cadence
- **Expressions:** Idioms, slang, characteristic phrases
- **Avoidances:** What this person would NEVER say
- **Code-switching:** How they adjust in different contexts

## 5. Historical & Period Research

### Period Film Research Framework
- **Visual World:** Architecture, fashion, technology, transportation
- **Social Norms:** Acceptable, taboo, aspirational
- **Daily Life:** Food, work, entertainment, communication
- **Language:** Period-appropriate speech (without overdoing it)
- **Power Structures:** Who had power? How exercised?
- **Emerging Tensions:** What was changing in society?
- **Anachronism Traps:** Common film mistakes about this era
- **Cinematic Opportunities:** Visually striking period details

### Historical Accuracy vs. Dramatic License
Determine the line:
- What historical facts are foundation? (Don't change)
- What gaps exist in the record? (Creative freedom zone)
- What can be compressed for drama? (Time, characters, events)
- What would historians flag? (Risk assessment)

## 6. The Story Bible Format

Compile all research into a single reference document.

```
# [PROJECT TITLE] — Story Bible

## World
- Setting, time period, locations
- Rules (physical, social, supernatural)
- Visual tone and palette

## Characters
- Full profiles for all speaking roles
- Relationship map
- Character voices (speech samples)

## Subject Matter
- Technical details and terminology
- Insider knowledge
- Verification notes (confirmed vs. assumed)

## Genre & Market
- Genre conventions to satisfy
- Comp titles and positioning
- Target audience profile
- Budget tier and production notes

## Research Sources
- Primary sources consulted
- Expert consultations needed
- Verification gaps to fill
```

The Story Bible is a living document — update throughout the writing process.
