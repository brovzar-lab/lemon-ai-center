# Save the Cat Structure

Page targets for 110-page screenplay. Adjust proportionally for other lengths.

---

## ACT ONE (Beats 1-10)

### Beat 1: OPENING IMAGE (p.1)

**Purpose:** Before-snapshot; tone + irony; seed theme. Visual that represents central struggle before adventure begins. Should mirror Final Image.

**Do this:** One visual contradiction that hints wound/theme; plant a prop/location to rhyme with Final Image.

**Watch out for:** Exposition/VO explaining feelings.

**Questions:** What single image screams the story's essence? How does it foreshadow the journey?

---

### Beats 2-4: SET-UP (pp.1-10)

Present protagonist's world as it is and what's missing. **Stasis = Death:** if life stays the same, protagonist figuratively dies. Reveal the flaw that needs fixing.

#### Set-Up #1 — Everyday Life
**Purpose:** Baseline routine the story will break.
**Do this:** Show a repeated action twice (pattern) and a micro-skill you'll later level up.
**Watch out for:** Errand scenes with no turn.

#### Set-Up #2 — Support Cast
**Purpose:** Define roles: mirror, foil, mentor, tempter.
**Do this:** Each entrance costs the hero something (time/status/$$); give every ally a want that will clash later.
**Watch out for:** Best friend who only nods and quips.

#### Set-Up #3 — World Resists Change
**Purpose:** Personify the system/status quo.
**Do this:** Show a rule and a penalty; preview the Act 2 arena.
**Watch out for:** Vague "society is hard" lines.

#### Plug-In: Home / Work / Play
Use these as content colors inside Beats 2-4:

**Home:** Show family dynamic shaping wound/desire; plant a relationship that carries stakes later.
- *Qs:* Who at home benefits if hero stays the same? Who's harmed if they change?

**Work:** Show competence and a ceiling; personify the system (boss/policy/client).
- *Qs:* How does the job echo the theme or hypocrisy?

**Play:** Hobby or refuge that foreshadows a tool used in Act 3.
- *Qs:* What skill from play will be repurposed at Beats 34-37?

---

### Beat 5: THEME STATED (p.5)

**Purpose:** Someone drops the thesis in throwaway form.

**Do this:** Put it inside conflict/snark; make it an actionable verb ("You can't ___ without ___"). Plan echoes at Midpoint and Finale.

**Watch out for:** Fortune-cookie on the nose.

**Questions:** What life lesson must the hero learn? How can it be posed as a challenge?

---

### Beat 6: CATALYST (p.12)

**Purpose:** Irreversible disruption that personally hits the wound.

The telegram, catching your loved-one cheating, allowing a monster onboard, a holographic message from a princess. The "before" world is no more.

**Do this:** Make the loss visible; remove a safety net.

**Watch out for:** Soft news hero could ignore.

---

### Beats 7-9: DEBATE (pp.12-25)

Change is scary. The hero doubts the journey. Can I face this? Do I have what it takes? Should I go at all?

#### Debate #1 — Hero Resists
**Purpose:** Old coping strategy fights back.
**Do this:** Let fear make smart arguments; end with the situation worse.
**Watch out for:** Montage of "thinking."

#### Debate #2 — Others Push
**Purpose:** External pressure vs. internal desire.
**Do this:** Two-hander argument with a clear win/lose turn; offer a credible alternative the hero rejects.
**Watch out for:** Cheerleading with no stakes.

#### Debate #3 — Waver
**Purpose:** Action becomes the lesser evil.
**Do this:** Add a clock and a personal stake (someone else gets hurt if the hero stalls).
**Watch out for:** Repeating #8 louder.

#### Plug-In: Debate at Work / Home / Play
**Work:** Risks/rewards of moving forward; clash with colleagues/superior triggers an early cost.
**Home:** Support vs. sabotage; personal sacrifice made explicit.
**Play:** Hobby reframed under pressure; metaphor that clarifies the choice.

---

### Beat 10: BREAK INTO TWO (p.25)

**Purpose:** Active choice across the threshold.

Hero makes a choice to go on the journey. We leave the "Thesis" world and enter the upside-down "Anti-thesis" world of Act Two.

**Do this:** Burn a bridge; show the cost. Shift texture/music: new rules apply.

**Watch out for:** Accident dragging hero forward.

---

## ACT TWO-A: FUN & GAMES (Beats 11-20)

### Beat 11: B STORY (p.30)

**Purpose:** Element/person that teaches the theme.

Discussion about the Theme. Often between protagonist and love interest, hence "love story." But can be any relationship or storyline that carries thematic weight.

**Do this:** Give B a conflicting worldview and a pact with the hero tied to A-stakes.

**Watch out for:** Comic relief only.

---

### Beats 12-16: FUN AND GAMES (pp.30-55)

**The Promise of the Premise.** Why the audience bought a ticket. The detective finds clues, Indiana Jones tracks the Ark, Harry & Sally are stuck together. This is the trailer section.

#### Fun & Games #1 — First Taste
**Purpose:** Deliver the premise at entry level.
**Do this:** Clear new rules; tiny surprising win.
**Watch out for:** Vague flailing.

#### Fun & Games #2 — Fumble
**Purpose:** Old strengths misfire under new rules.
**Do this:** Cause-and-effect gag/chaos chain; ally bails hero (to reverse later).
**Watch out for:** Same gag re-skinned.

#### Fun & Games #3 — Scale Up
**Purpose:** Showcase at bigger scope; plant tool for Act 3.
**Do this:** Success → hubris OR failure → doubt; leave a plant.
**Watch out for:** Random set piece not tied to goal.

#### Fun & Games #4 — World Reacts
**Purpose:** Others recalibrate to hero; system notices.
**Do this:** Status up/downs for allies; quantifiable consequence.
**Watch out for:** Crowd applause with no cost.

#### Fun & Games #5 — Poster Moment
**Purpose:** Trailer image with a price tag.
**Do this:** Clean objective + visible choice; end with cost that points to Midpoint.
**Watch out for:** Empty spectacle.

#### Set Piece Structure
Each set piece: **Objective → New Rule → Complication → Button (cost/plant)**
Ensure at least one creates a poster image and leaves a plant paid at 30/34/37.

---

### Beats 17-18: MIDPOINT APPROACH

#### Beat 17 — Midpoint Set-Up
**Purpose:** Tighten screws; redefine stakes.
**Do this:** Reveal truer opponent/system; put something non-resettable on the clock.
**Watch out for:** Wheel-spinning.

---

### Beat 18: MIDPOINT (p.55)

**Purpose:** Public + personal flip; fun → cost.

Largest plot twist. Raises stakes, can change the goal completely, or makes requirements much more difficult. Can feel like a new movie is starting. Hero must recommit. Often B Story incites the twist. Often a "ticking time clock" begins here.

**Do this:** Win that's poisoned / loss that clarifies; change power/knowledge. False victory or false defeat.

**Watch out for:** A twist that could be cut without ripple.

---

### Beats 19-21: MIDPOINT AFTERMATH

#### Beat 19 — Shock
**Purpose:** Disorientation.
**Do this:** Remove an ally/resource; hero misreads the new reality.
**Watch out for:** Replaying Midpoint.

#### Beat 20 — New Dangers
**Purpose:** Villain/system counters smarter.
**Do this:** Shift terrain/rules; corner hero into a bad bargain.
**Watch out for:** Same obstacle, louder.

#### Beat 21 — Relationship Shift
**Purpose:** Bonds strain/break; theme voiced by B.
**Do this:** Confession or betrayal; set fuse for All Is Lost.
**Watch out for:** Heart-to-heart with no turn.

---

## ACT TWO-B: BAD GUYS CLOSE IN (Beats 22-25)

Now Fun & Games get serious. Hero's gifts are no use. Plans foiled. Team splits. Trusted friend betrays. The detective is now being chased, Indiana Jones is thrown into a snake pit, Harry runs into his ex and takes it out on Sally.

#### Beat 22 — External Bad Guys
**Purpose:** Antagonists start winning.
**Do this:** Territory lost; trap/surveillance the hero doesn't see.
**Watch out for:** Off-screen threats only.

#### Beat 23 — Internal Bad Guys
**Purpose:** Wound relapse hurts team/B story.
**Do this:** Hero chooses wrong under pressure; cost lands on an ally.
**Watch out for:** Pure victimhood.

#### Beat 24 — Ironic Echo
**Purpose:** Revisit Act 1 flaw/location; regression.
**Do this:** Same choice made badly; twist Theme line against hero.
**Watch out for:** Easter egg with no function.

#### Beat 25 — Ticking Clock
**Purpose:** Lock the cage.
**Do this:** Hard deadline/resource drain; visible cost of failure; compromise that sets up the fall.
**Watch out for:** Timer that never forces choices.

**Exterior/Interior Pattern:** Alternate external pressure (antagonists, environment, institutions) with internal pressure (fear, shame, addiction, pride—the wound relapsing) so it feels compounded, not repetitive.

---

## ACT TWO-C: ALL IS LOST / DARK NIGHT (Beats 26-30)

### Beat 26: ALL IS LOST (p.75)

**Purpose:** Devastating blow caused by hero's choices.

The low point. Hero loses everything gained or realizes everything they have has no meaning. They've lost even more than if they'd never gone on the journey. **Whiff of Death:** something or someone dies (literal or metaphorical). The death of something old makes way for something new.

**Do this:** Use death imagery (literal/symbolic); strip supports (money/allies/leverage).

**Watch out for:** Meteor-from-nowhere. Must be caused by hero's flaw/choices.

---

### Beat 27: WHIFF OF DEATH

**Purpose:** Sit with consequence and mortality.

**Do this:** Stillness; tactile token of loss (ring/badge/photo).

**Watch out for:** Melodrama without specificity.

---

### Beats 28-29: DARK NIGHT OF THE SOUL (pp.75-85)

Hero hits bottom. Wallows in hopelessness. "Why hast thou forsaken me?" Mourning the loss of what has "died"—the dream, the goal, the mentor, the love of your life. Must fall completely before picking up and trying again. **Here the arc completes as hero fully understands the truth they couldn't see when they set out.**

#### Beat 28 — Process Despair (Interior)
**Purpose:** Honest low.
**Do this:** Hero states the wrong story about self; no new info yet—only recognition.
**Watch out for:** Inspirational rando pep talks.

#### Beat 29 — Theme Callback (Interior/Exterior Bridge)
**Purpose:** Reframe Theme line.
**Do this:** B-story artifact (memory/voicemail) returns; hero challenges previous belief.
**Watch out for:** Magic epiphany.

**Exterior Fallout:** Tangible losses (status, legal, safety).
**Interior Reckoning:** Reframing the Theme; what belief must die for the hero to live?

---

### Beat 30: BREAKTHROUGH TO ACT THREE

**Purpose:** Insight from growth, not luck.

DNOTS directly followed by "New Information" also delivered via B Story. This functions as a second Catalyst giving the hero the choice again—pack up and go home, or give it one more try.

**Do this:** Connect two earlier plants; the solution costs the thing they feared most; mark recommitment visually.

**Watch out for:** New character strolling in with the answer.

---

## ACT THREE (Beats 31-40)

### Beat 31: BREAK INTO THREE (p.85)

**Purpose:** Fuse A + B; state the objective.

Thanks to fresh idea, new inspiration, or last-minute thematic advice from B Story (usually love interest), hero chooses to try again. The New Information presents the final goal.

**Do this:** Ask for help the hero wouldn't in Act 1; define success/failure conditions.

**Watch out for:** Plan we can't follow.

---

### 5-POINT FINALE (Beats 32-38)

Hero confronts antagonist with new strength. Incorporates Theme—the nugget of truth that now makes sense—because they have experience from A Story and context from B Story. Hero has left "Anti-thesis" world and entered "Synthesis" world.

#### Beat 32 — Gather Team & Supplies
**Purpose:** Leadership beat; roles that test new values.
**Do this:** Assign jobs; include one refusal that forces a pivot; make stakes public.
**Watch out for:** Generic pep talk.

#### Beat 33 — Execute the Plan
**Purpose:** Stepwise mechanics.
**Do this:** Show checkpoints, a planned sacrifice, and one expected failure you'll solve later.
**Watch out for:** Action mush with no objectives.

#### Beat 34 — Mirror Test
**Purpose:** Face an earlier failure and pass using new behavior.
**Do this:** Rhyming location/situation; win access, not victory yet.
**Watch out for:** Unrelated obstacle.

#### Beat 35 — Henchmen/Trials
**Purpose:** Stress teamwork and resource scarcity.
**Do this:** Each ally's role matters; time pressure visible.
**Watch out for:** Endless punching.

#### Beat 36 — High-Tower Surprise (Impossible Wall)
**Purpose:** Plan breaks; stakes spike.
**Do this:** Reveal hidden truth/reversal; hero must risk what they vowed never to risk.
**Watch out for:** Deus ex machina rescue.

#### Beat 37 — Dig Deep / Showdown
**Purpose:** Win by theme, not brute force.
**Do this:** Choice between two goods/evils; pay a real cost; weaponize the Theme to beat the antagonist.
**Watch out for:** Villain slips on banana peel.

**Note:** The "New Plan" is not a new beat—it's the pivot between 36→37 where the hero applies the theme to adapt and win.

---

### Beat 38: FINALE RESOLUTION — PROOF

**Purpose:** Tie up action threads with visible outcomes.

**Do this:** World reaction; cash in plants; leave one deliberate aftertaste thread.

**Watch out for:** Overlong epilogue.

---

### Beat 39: FINAL IMAGE SETUP — NEW NORMAL

**Purpose:** Contrast to Opening Image.

**Do this:** Same composition, key difference; secondary character silently confirms change.

**Watch out for:** Text card explainers.

---

### Beat 40: FINAL IMAGE (p.110)

**Purpose:** Wordless proof that the Theme is now true.

Visual proof of transformation. Mirrors Opening Image but shows change.

**Do this:** Close the visual rhyme from Beat 1; one clean emotional note (earned).

**Watch out for:** Hallmark platitude.

---

## GLOBAL CRAFT RULES

**Cause → Effect:** Every beat must cause the next beat. Draw arrows. If you can remove a beat and nothing breaks, cut it.

**Theme as Verb:** Phrase it like "___ requires ___." Stress-test at Beats 5 → 18 → 37 → 40.

**Plant/Payoff Map:** Plants in Beats 12-16 must pay at 30/31/34/37/38.

**Resource Depletion Curve:** Allies/time/$$ decline 22-26; scarcity forces ingenuity 31-37.

**Public vs. Private Stakes:** Explode both at Beat 18; at Beat 37 the hero risks the private to secure the public (or vice-versa).

**Scene Polarity (+/-):** Mark +/– for every scene. No change = no scene.

**Page Guardrails (110p baseline):**
- Catalyst ~12
- Break into Two ~25
- Midpoint ~55
- All Is Lost ~75
- Break into Three ~85

Miss the function, not the minute.

**No Redundant Beats:** Home/Work/Play are colors inside Set-Up/Debate, not extra beats. Same for Exterior/Interior inside Bad Guys/Dark Night.

## BRUTAL CHECKLIST

- If Beat 6 (Catalyst) isn't truly irreversible, Beat 10 won't feel earned.
- If Beat 18 (Midpoint) doesn't change power/knowledge, Beats 22-25 will feel like repetition.
- If Beat 26 (All Is Lost) isn't caused by the hero's flaw/choices, Beats 28-30 read as self-pity.
- If Beat 37 (Dig Deep) isn't a thematic act with a paid cost, Beat 40 is hollow.

## STC GENRE TYPES

Match structural beats to genre expectations:

1. **Monster in the House** — Enclosed space + sin that brings monster + characters picked off
2. **Golden Fleece** — Road story + team + each location tests hero
3. **Out of the Bottle** — Wish granted + lesson learned + return to normal
4. **Dude with a Problem** — Ordinary person + extraordinary circumstance
5. **Rites of Passage** — Life transition + wrong way to handle it + acceptance
6. **Buddy Love** — Incomplete hero + counterpart + surrender to relationship
7. **Whydunit** — Crime/mystery + dark turn that implicates all
8. **Fool Triumphant** — Underdog + establishment + transmutation
9. **Institutionalized** — Group + choice to join/burn/compromise
10. **Superhero** — Special power + nemesis + curse of being special
