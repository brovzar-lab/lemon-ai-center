# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Billy Rovzar, CEO of Lemon Studios. Solo user. Works from a desktop during the business day, managing a film/entertainment production company. Needs to triage email, prep for meetings, track tasks, and make decisions — all without context-switching between five apps.

## Product Purpose

A single command center that consolidates Gmail, Google Calendar, Notion, and AI-powered skills into one calm surface. Success looks like: Billy opens this once in the morning, knows exactly what matters today, and handles 80% of his daily workflow without leaving the dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Calm, organized, easy to follow. The voice of a trusted executive assistant — never loud, never vague. Warm confidence without showmanship.

## Anti-references

- Not a generic SaaS dashboard (HubSpot, Salesforce) — no metric cards, no conversion funnels, no "engagement" widgets
- Not a neon-on-black crypto terminal — no aggressive gradients, no hacker aesthetic
- Not busy, not overwhelming, not disorganized — no wall-of-cards, no competing CTAs, no visual noise
- Not flat-white admin (Notion, Google Admin) — the warmth and editorial tone are intentional, not decorative

## Design Principles

1. **One thing at a time** — every screen answers one question clearly before introducing the next. Hierarchy is ruthless.
2. **Quiet confidence** — the interface knows what's important so Billy doesn't have to figure it out. Intelligence is felt, not displayed.
3. **Warmth over sterility** — the Lemon Studios brand is cinematic and human. The dashboard should feel like a well-made leather notebook, not a spreadsheet.
4. **Earn every element** — if a component doesn't reduce cognitive load or accelerate a decision, it doesn't belong on screen.
5. **Respect the morning** — the first 30 seconds set the tone. Morning state is the most important state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Reduced-motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Sufficient contrast ratios on the warm-dark palette (4.5:1 minimum for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components). No information conveyed by color alone.
